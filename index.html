Yuri Gagarin

First human in space.

"I see Earth! It is so beautiful!"

Here's a time line of Yuri Gagarin's life:

  • 9 March 1934 - Born in Klushino, Russia
  • 1946 - Family moves to Gzhatsk, Russia, where Gagarin continued his education.
  • 1955 - Gagarin was accepted to the First Chkalovsky Higher Air Force Pilots School in Orenburg
  • 1957 - Gagarin was commissioned a lieutenant in the Soviet Air Forces having accumulated 166 hours and 47 minutes of flight time.
  • 12 April 1961 - Vostok 1 spacecraft was launched from Baikonur Cosmodrome. Aboard was Gagarin, the first human to travel into space.
  • 1962 - Became a lieutenant colonel of the Soviet Air Forces.
  • 1963 - Received the rank of colonel.
  • 1965 - Yuri began to re-qualify as a fighter pilot and was backup pilot for his friend Vladimir Komarov on the Soyuz 1 flight after five years without piloting duty.
  • 1966 - He was replaced by Komarov and reassigned to Soyuz 3.
  • 17 February 1968 - Gagarin successfully defended his aerospace engineering thesis on the subject of spaceplane aerodynamic configuration and graduated cum laude from the Zhukovsky Air Force Engineering Academy.
  • 27 March 1968 - dies at the age of 34.
